Product Design EE SIM-only

Simplifying data plan selection

Role
Senior Product Designer
Team
SIM-only acquisition team
Duration
EE / BT Consumer
Impact
29% faster task completion, 8% sales uplift

The situation

EE’s SIM-only plan selection journey wasn’t serving its customers well. Plans were laid out in ways that made comparison hard, the Smart Benefits offering was buried, and users couldn’t easily tell what they were getting for the price difference between tiers. From a commercial perspective, the team also wanted customers to recognise the value of higher-tier plans — not to push people up the ladder, but to make sure customers who’d genuinely benefit from a better plan could see why.

What I did

I led the redesign of EE’s data plan cards as Senior Product Designer on the SIM-only acquisition team, working closely with content designer Emma Paines and the wider product, brand, and marketing teams. We ran interviews, analytics deep-dives, and workshops to understand where comparison was breaking down. I led ideation and testing sessions to rework layout, hierarchy, and content, simplifying the decision while still supporting the upsell objective.

An array of the validated plan card variants. Content design by Emma Paines, product design by me.

The result

29% faster task completion in moderated usability testing, an 8% sales uplift after rollout, and a measurable shift toward higher-value plans. The last one is the outcome I’d point a Principal interviewer at: shifting customers up the value ladder through clearer information design rather than dark patterns is a Lead-level problem to solve well. Customers were buying better-fitting plans, not getting nudged into ones they didn’t need.

What I took from this

Information design with commercial intent is where UX and product strategy meet. Most designers can lift conversion. Fewer know how to lift average order value through clarity — without sacrificing the user’s trust. That balance is the work.
