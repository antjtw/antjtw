UI Designer
Team

Impact
Modernised the experience; contributed to Sky's Polaris design system

The situation

Sky’s Broadband & Talk Hub on My Account was the page customers used to check their connection, troubleshoot issues, and manage their service. It was tired. The information architecture didn’t prioritise the tasks customers actually came to do, and the visual treatment didn’t fit the new My Account design language. The brief was to modernise it and align it with the wider system.

What I did

I worked as UI Designer alongside UX designer Francesco Martino. My role bridged UX and UI, contributing to design sprints, facilitating workshops, and turning user research insights into actionable design decisions. I partnered with contact-centre teams to surface the real-world pain points that weren’t showing up in the analytics, then restructured the information architecture around the tasks customers asked for most: troubleshooting and billing.

Alongside the project work, I contributed to Sky’s Polaris design system — specifically the components that the Broadband & Talk experience helped to refine and standardise.

The dev-ready Broadband & Talk experience. UX design by Francesco Martino, UI design by me.

The result

The redesigned experience shipped as dev-ready. It simplified complex broadband data into something a non-technical customer could read and act on, and the patterns we proved fed back into Polaris for the rest of My Account to use. This was earlier in my career and the work that taught me design systems aren’t a side artefact — they’re the thing that determines whether one team’s craft becomes the whole org’s baseline.
