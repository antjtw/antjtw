Allowing Trusts and Hospitals to report COVID-19 test results

The final bulk upload flow before service decommission (reimagined with 2025 GOV.UK UI). Content design by Fergus Alexander, interaction design and prototype by me.

Role
Senior Interaction Designer
Team
Elective Care Test & Trace, via Accenture
Duration
Jun 2022 – Nov 2022
Impact
80% reduction in average reporting times across NHS Trusts

The situation

NHS Trusts and hospitals needed to report COVID-19 test results to central government, but the existing process was manual, slow, and inconsistent. During an active pandemic, every hour of delay between a result landing and the data reaching public health teams was a direct cost to the response. The Elective Care Test & Trace team commissioned a service redesign as part of the wider GOV.UK programme.

What I did

I joined as Senior Interaction Designer, working alongside content designers, service designers, policy and delivery leads. My focus was rapid, accessible prototyping using the GOV.UK Prototyping Tool, and iterative usability testing against real NHS staff. The work spanned individual-patient flows and a bulk-upload route for higher-volume Trusts.

I extended the GDS Design System where the standard patterns didn’t quite fit clinical workflows, and contributed those extensions back upstream so the next team to need them wouldn’t have to rebuild. Everything had to meet GDS accessibility standards, which is a non-negotiable bar in government service design.

The bulk upload and individual-patient reporting flows. Content design by Fergus Alexander, interaction design and prototype by me.

The result

The redesigned flows cut average reporting times by 80% across the participating Trusts. The service was decommissioned in line with the wider pandemic response winding down; the design patterns we’d shipped back to GDS persisted past the service itself, which is the right kind of legacy for a piece of government work.
